Motto: Safe and considerate utilization of the landscape lies in respect for the natural processes that shape it.

The major part of human activity is bound to the surface or its immediate surroundings. This means that the nature of the earth’s surface (morphology, geology, land cover etc.) and the processes it creates have an immediate impact on human activity and the manner of land use. It follows that understanding the evolution of the earth’s surface is an essential prerequisite for its effective and environmentally considerate use.

Geomorphological terrain analysis

Characteristics of geomorphological terrain analysis

Geomorphological terrain analysis is based on the demarcation of individual landforms and the assessment of their formation and approximate age. The analysis is based primarily on the interpretation of a digital terrain model. This study can be supplemented with terrain documentation or the evaluation of satellite and aerial images.

Geomorphological map as an output of geomorphological terrain analysis

The geomorphological map represents a generalized image of the area’s development. Circumscribed landforms are subsequently divided into groups specified by their origin: i) structural and tectonic landforms, ii) landforms of denudation, iii) landforms of accumulation, iv) anthropogenic landforms. Individual landforms are drawn on a suitable map base in the form of polygons, lines, and eventual point markers. The scale of the map affects the extent of detail.

Practical use of geomorphological terrain analysis

The results of geomorphological terrain analysis serve as basic data needed for analysing the vulnerability of the territory to natural processes such as floods, erosion, landslides, earthquakes, and others.

Analysis of natural hazards

Evaluation of the vulnerability of the territory by natural processes

The expanding urbanisation of the countryside and infrastructure development lead to continual interaction between human activity and various natural processes. Underestimating the impact of this interaction can result in extensive damage such as landslides, floods, or earthquakes. The importance of analysing the vulnerability of the territory lies in locating and characterizing those natural processes that present acute or potential hazards.

The use of natural hazards analysis

Prevention — using the outputs to create new spatial plans, environmental plans, developer and construction projects, and plans for civil defence.

Solution of current problems — using the outputs to propose remedial and security measures that would reduce the danger of soil erosion, landslides, floods, and others.
